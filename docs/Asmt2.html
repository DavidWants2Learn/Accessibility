About Me

As a student veteran I understand the importance of being cooperative in a team.
Each individual contributes to a team in a unique way,
and it is this that makes the sum of the whole that makes a dynamic system function.
During my time in the US Army, I was physically weaker but more compassionate than
most of my team members. I felt as if I weren’t able to contribute as much as my teammates.
I eventually noticed that the local’s in Iraq felt less threatened
by me and would share information that kept my platoon safe.

I am a driven individual whose been set back by personal and financial circumstances,
but i’ve never given up on my education. After leaving the military,
I had a difficult time adjusting back into society, and my financial circumstances
made it almost impossible to continue my education. However, I persevered
by making some sacrifices such as renting a living room, sleeping on couches,
and eating cheap to make it possible.

I’ve been told that I am a diligent worker. Once I get into the zone,
I don’t stop working until I am satisfied with the outcome.
One of the most difficult projects that I worked on was creating a
digital safe using the ARM Assembly language. Although it took me over
2 weeks of non-stop learning, and coding. I was finally able to complete the project.
The professor later noted that I was the only person that completed the project
in my own unique way, without referencing the examples that were available online.

What I hope to gain in this course is to be able empathize with the user
by developing intuitive applications that are simple and easy to use.
